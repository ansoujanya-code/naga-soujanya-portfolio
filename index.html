About Me

Built for the intersection of
data, AI, and people.

I'm a first-semester graduate student in AI and Data Analytics at Indiana Wesleyan University, driven by a simple conviction: data should serve people, not just optimize systems. My background bridges technical analysis with communication and leadership, which means I care as much about how insights are delivered as how they're derived.

Before this program, I worked with data in applied settings where I learned that the hardest part of analytics is rarely the math — it's the change management. Convincing a team to trust a model, redesigning workflows around new tools, and navigating resistance: those are the skills that actually move organizations forward.

I'm passionate about ethical AI, adaptive leadership, and building systems where humans and algorithms work together honestly. My goal is to become a data leader who asks "Is this right?" just as readily as "Does this work?"
